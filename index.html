Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between var, let, and const?
var is function-scoped, let is block-scoped, and const is also block-scoped but cannot be reassigned
What is the difference between map(), forEach(), and filter()?
map() returns a new array, forEach() iterates without returning, and filter() returns an array with elements that match a condition.
Explain arrow functions and how they are different from regular functions.
Arrow functions in JavaScript provide a shorter syntax for writing functions and do not have their own "this", instead inheriting it from their surrounding scope. Unlike regular functions, they cannot be used as constructors, lack "arguments", and do not have their own "this" binding, making them ideal for callbacks and functional programming.
How do JavaScript Promises work?
Promises handle asynchronous operations by representing a value that may be available now, later, or never.
How do closures work in JavaScript?
A closure is a function that retains access to its lexical scope even when executed outside that scope.
